creek

[NOUN] a member of a confederacy of American Indian peoples of the south-eastern US in the 16th to 19th centuries; their descendants now live mainly in Oklahoma.
[NOUN] the Muskogean language that was spoken by members of the Creek confederacy.
[ADJECTIVE] relating to or denoting the Creek or their language.
[NOUN] a narrow, sheltered waterway, especially an inlet in a shoreline or channel in a marsh.
